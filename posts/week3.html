3D Printing — Machine Selection & First Bottom Casings

📅 27 February 2026 ⏱ 6 min read 🏷 FDM · Print Parameters · Prototyping

Choosing a Process and Machine

This week marks the transition from observation to production. The immediate task is to manufacture the first prototypes of the bottom casing using the engineering drawing provided by Phones-R-Us. Before touching a machine, however, a decision must be made about which additive process to use and why. The lab offers access to multiple printer types; selecting the right one for this application is itself an engineering judgement.

The primary candidates are Fused Deposition Modelling (FDM) and Stereolithography (SLA / resin) printing. FDM offers lower material cost, a wider range of structural polymers (PLA, PETG, ABS), and familiarity for most operators. SLA produces significantly higher surface resolution and tighter dimensional accuracy but introduces post-processing steps (washing, UV curing) that extend the effective cycle time. For a phone casing — a thin-walled, snap-fit component requiring reasonable surface finish and good dimensional fidelity — both processes are plausible candidates.
