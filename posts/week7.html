Tecnomatix Plant Simulation — Building the Virtual Assembly Line

📅 3 April 2026 ⏱ 7 min read 🏷 Tecnomatix · Digital Twin · Simulation

From Physical to Virtual

The observations made in Week 2 at the FESTO CP lab now take on a new purpose: they are the input data for a digital twin of the phone assembly process. Using Siemens Tecnomatix Plant Simulation, I will construct a process-level model of the six-station assembly line, simulate its operation, and extract throughput and cycle time data. This model will then be used in Week 8 to answer the critical question: can this system — supplemented by an appropriate fleet of 3D printers — produce 50,000 phones per year?

The value of simulation in this context is well established. As Negahban and Smith (2014) note, discrete-event simulation allows manufacturers to experiment with system configurations, staffing levels, and process parameters without the cost and disruption of physical trials. In an Industry 4.0 framework, such simulation forms a key component of the digital thread — the continuous flow of information linking design, production planning, and manufacturing execution (Stark, 2015).
