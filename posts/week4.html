Metrology & Scanning — Are Our Parts in Tolerance?

📅 6 March 2026 ⏱ 5 min read 🏷 Metrology · GD&T · 3D Scanning

Why Measurement Matters

Producing a part is only half the challenge. Knowing whether that part meets its dimensional specification — and therefore whether it will function correctly within the assembly — requires a systematic measurement strategy. This week we move from the print room to the metrology suite, bringing with us the batch of bottom casings produced last week and the measurement plan I drew up from the engineering drawing.

The fundamental challenge of dimensional verification in additive manufacturing is well documented in the literature. Unlike CNC machining, where cutting forces produce relatively predictable deviations, FDM parts can exhibit anisotropic shrinkage, layer-to-layer inconsistency, and surface roughness that complicates both contact and non-contact measurement (Minetola et al., 2016). Understanding these error sources is essential before we can have any confidence in the fitness of our casings for the FESTO assembly line.
