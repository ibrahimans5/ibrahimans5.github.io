First Look at the FESTO CP Lab — 6 Stations Observed

📅 20 February 2026 ⏱ 5 min read 🏷 FESTO · Assembly Line · Cycle Time

Entering the Lab

This week we had our first hands-on session with the FESTO CP Lab — the physical phone assembly system that forms the backbone of the Phones-R-Us scenario. The lab replicates, at a reduced scale, the kind of modular conveyor-and-station layout found in consumer electronics manufacturing. Seeing it in operation for the first time was genuinely instructive: the sequence of operations, the pacing between stations, and the moments where parts must be presented in precisely the right orientation all became immediately legible in a way that a textbook description cannot replicate.

The system consists of six sequential stations, each performing a discrete step in the phone assembly process. My task today was to observe carefully, time each station, and document what I saw in enough detail to later construct a faithful digital model in Tecnomatix Plant Simulation. Below I break down each station.

Implications for Simulation

The observations from this session will directly inform the Tecnomatix model I will build in Week 7. Particularly important are the individual station cycle times, which I have recorded in my lab notebook and will tabulate once I have had a chance to cross-check them across multiple production cycles. As Mourtzis, Doukas and Bernidaki (2014) note, simulation fidelity is strongly dependent on the quality of input data; rough estimates derived from single observations can introduce significant error into throughput predictions.

The FESTO system also made tangible the importance of dimensional consistency in the casings I will produce.
